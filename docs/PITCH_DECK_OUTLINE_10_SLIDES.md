# Pitch Deck Outline (10 Slides)

Use case: Founder/Investor presentation
Reference: `docs/ONE_PAGER_FOUNDER_INVESTOR.md`

## Slide 1: Title and Vision
- Title: eLekha - Operating Layer for Small Business and Personal Finance
- Subtitle: One mobile workflow for transactions, dues, stock, and decisions
- Show:
  - Brand name and one-line promise
  - Founder name and contact
- Speaker note:
  - Position eLekha as daily-use financial operations app, not just accounting software.

## Slide 2: Problem
- Title: The Daily Money Management Problem
- Show:
  - Tools are fragmented (billing, stock, dues, expenses in separate places)
  - Owners lose time, miss collections, and make late decisions
  - Teams lack controlled access
- Speaker note:
  - Emphasize operational pain, not only bookkeeping pain.

## Slide 3: Solution
- Title: One App, Connected Workflows
- Show:
  - Business: POS, Orders, Billing, Inventory, Ledger, Reports, Team Roles
  - Personal: Income/Expense, Budget, EMI/Loans, Notes, Tax Calculator
  - Connected data updates dashboards and reports automatically
- Speaker note:
  - Highlight that users do not need separate apps for daily operations.

## Slide 4: Product Walkthrough
- Title: How eLekha Works in a Day
- Show (simple flow):
  1. Setup contacts/products/accounts
  2. Run POS/orders and record payments
  3. Track due and stock
  4. Review dashboard and reports
- Speaker note:
  - Demonstrate real operational loop in less than 30 seconds.

## Slide 5: Why We Win
- Title: Differentiation
- Show:
  - Business + Personal in one app context
  - Role-based view/manage permissions
  - Cross-feature connected data (less manual reconciliation)
  - Mobile-first speed for field and counter use
- Speaker note:
  - Focus on practical differentiation, not abstract AI claims.

## Slide 6: User and Market
- Title: Target Users and Expansion Path
- Show:
  - Primary: small retail/service businesses
  - Secondary: operators/accountants and personal users
  - Expansion: multi-user teams, multi-location operations
- Speaker note:
  - Show that initial wedge can expand into broader financial ops stack.

## Slide 7: Business Model
- Title: Monetization Strategy
- Show:
  - Freemium base for activation
  - Paid tiers for advanced reports, role control, automation, and workflows
  - Potential add-ons: advanced reminders, insights, exports, compliance packs
- Speaker note:
  - Explain value-based pricing linked to operational outcomes.

## Slide 8: Traction and KPI Framework
- Title: What We Measure
- Show:
  - Time to first transaction
  - Weekly active accounts (Business vs Personal)
  - Transactions/orders per active account
  - Overdue reduction and collection efficiency
  - 30-day retention
- Speaker note:
  - If live traction numbers are not ready, show this as reporting framework and baseline plan.

## Slide 9: Roadmap (Next 2 Quarters)
- Title: Execution Roadmap
- Show:
  - Q1: onboarding clarity, permission messaging, stable core flows
  - Q2: insights, reminders, unified search, timeline visibility
  - Quality gates: cross-feature consistency, role safety, currency consistency
- Speaker note:
  - Communicate disciplined shipping with user-value priorities.

## Slide 10: Ask and Close
- Title: What We Need and Why Now
- Show:
  - Funding/partnership ask
  - Where capital/resources will be used (product, GTM, support)
  - Milestones to next round (activation, retention, paid conversion)
- Speaker note:
  - End with clear ask and measurable outcomes.

## Optional Appendix Slides
1. Competitive comparison matrix
2. Deeper product architecture view (non-technical)
3. Detailed unit economics assumptions
4. Feature adoption by cohort

## Presenter Checklist
1. Keep total pitch to 8-12 minutes.
2. Use one customer story through slides 2-4.
3. Show numbers where available; avoid generic claims.
4. End with a specific ask and timeline.
