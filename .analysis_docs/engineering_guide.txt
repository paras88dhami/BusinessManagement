1. Executive engineering summary
The current UI prototype already shows most of the product surface area: auth, account setup, home, ledger, transactions, EMI & loans, profile, accounts, products, inventory, reports, categories, settings, POS, contacts, billing, user management, and subscription. For production, however, the app must stop treating those as one flat mixed set of pages. The implementation must resolve context first and then load the correct Personal or Business experience.
• Current risk: the home shell and profile flow are business-heavy while the app is intended to support both Personal and Business worlds.
• Current risk: the auth UI still exposes email input and social auth options, while the lock list fixes v2.1 auth to phone number + password and explicitly keeps social auth out of launch scope.
• Current risk: subscription naming and entitlement packaging are inconsistent across existing materials. Billing logic must use entitlement keys rather than hard-coded marketing strings until the final pricing sheet is locked.
• Production requirement: every persistent record must carry explicit ownership scope. No silent fallback to a random business, random account, or last-seen state is allowed.

Core implementation stance
The engineering team should treat the current repo as a UI prototype and interaction map, not as the final architecture. The implementation target is a production app whose routing, storage, domain boundaries, and APIs are built around explicit context resolution and domain ownership.

4. Final routing and context resolution flow
• Startup gate: load language, app settings, secure session, active profile, current mode, active business, and active account in order.
• No session: route to auth.
• Session but no profile: route to profile selector or profile creation.
• Session and profile but no mode: route to mode selector.
• Business mode without business context: route to business selector or business creation.
• Resolved personal context: route to personal home.
• Resolved business context: route to business home.
• Context change: switching profile, mode, business, or account must refresh the active shell, invalidate scoped caches, and reload dashboard widgets.

7. Backend/API principles
• Every request must be scope-aware. The backend should never infer business scope from a route string alone.
• Use entitlement keys, not plan names. Example keys: can_use_pos, can_export_reports, max_staff_count.
• Keep Transactions, Ledger, and EMI separate. Reports may consume a read-model that unifies them, but write APIs stay separate.
• Audit trail is mandatory for destructive operations. Edits, deletes, reversals, voids, and forced closures require actor, time, and reason.
• Support offline-safe sync design. Conflicts should resolve by entity versioning and scoped ownership, not by last-write blind overwrite.
